Why Eonix Exists

Modern embedded systems are not failing because of complexity — they’re failing because of fragmentation.

Messy wiring. Multiple microcontrollers doing overlapping jobs. I²C conflicts. Debugging blind systems with no visibility. Power that isn’t controlled, just supplied. Systems that work in the lab but break under real conditions.

What should be a system becomes a collection of hacks.

Eonix exists to eliminate this entire class of problems — by treating hardware as a unified system, not separate parts.

Power Stage

Controlled, programmable power with hardware-enforced protection.

Not just supply — regulated, monitored, and stable under real loads.

Sensor Stage

Structured sensing with deterministic communication.

No address conflicts. No protocol chaos. Just clean, system-level data.

Actuator Stage

High-current control designed for real-world loads.

Built for precise control — not just switching signals on and off.
